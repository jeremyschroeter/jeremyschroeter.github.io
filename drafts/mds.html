Multidimensional scaling (MDS) is one of the most common and simplest forms of dimensionality reduction. One reason why it is so simple is because the problem statement is very intuitive.

The Problem Statement

Suppose we are given an n \times n matrix \mathbf{D} containing distances between n points.

D_{ij} = \Vert \mathbf{x}_i - \mathbf{x}_j\Vert \qquad \mathbf{x} \in \mathbb{R}^p
